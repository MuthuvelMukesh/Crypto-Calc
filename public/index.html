TDS & Compliance Guide

Important information for Indian crypto tax compliance

üìã Form 26QB - TDS on Crypto Sales

Applicable from August 1, 2023

TDS Rate: 1% on sale value (when threshold crossed in same financial year)

Threshold Limits (per FY):

  • Individual/HUF: ‚Çπ50,00,000 (‚Çπ50 lakh)
  • Others: ‚Çπ10,00,000 (‚Çπ10 lakh)

Who Deducts: Crypto exchange, broker, or facilitator

Filing Requirement: Exchanges must file Form 26QB quarterly

üéØ VDA Tax (Section 94A) - 30% Flat Tax

Applicability: On capital gains from Virtual Digital Assets (VDA)

Tax Rate: 30% on gross gain (cannot claim deductions)

Health & Education Cess: 4% on tax + surcharge

Surcharge: Based on total income bracket

Important: Losses cannot be set off against other income or carried forward

‚ö†Ô∏è Each transaction is treated separately for tax purposes

üìä Cost of Acquisition

What Counts as Cost:

  • Purchase price of the asset
  • Brokerage & exchange fees (on purchase)
  • GST on fees
  • Any transfer/import costs

Costing Method Options:

  • FIFO: First-in, First-out (most common)
  • Average Cost: Weighted average per unit

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls

  • ‚ùå Not reporting crypto trades: Exchanges report to income tax department
  • ‚ùå Forgetting bank withdrawal fees: These are deductible from gains
  • ‚ùå Ignoring FY boundaries: Report by Financial Year (Apr-Mar), not calendar year
  • ‚ùå Missing TDS certificates: Get Form 16A from exchange for tax filing
  • ‚ùå Mixing up transaction costs: Separate purchase and sale fees
  • ‚ùå Not tracking cost basis: Keep complete records of all purchases

üìù Documentation & Records

Keep these records for 6+ years:

  • Exchange statements & transaction history
  • Screenshots of buy/sell confirmations
  • Bank statements showing deposits/withdrawals
  • Email confirmations from exchanges
  • Form 26QB from exchanges (TDS certificate)
  • Cost basis calculations (FIFOor Average)
  • Schedule FA (Capital Gains Schedule) from this calculator
